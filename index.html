About



I started drawing before I could speak, and have not been able to stop since. My work in traditional art is primarily mixed media. I make use of dip pens, oil paint, acrylics, watercolor paint, crayons, paper cutouts and found objects. I've had my paintings and sculptures featured in galleries throughout the US.

I have worked in graphic design and web design since 2003 and continually strive to learn new technologies. I specialize in creating web sites, logotypes and logos that truly reflect the people and organizations they are made for.

I first started using *nix based operating systems in 1998. I wanted an alternative to the other operating systems in use at the time, and made the jump after seeing a freebsd box. My love of free open source software and commandline tools has only grown since then.

Affilliations



I frequently volunteer my time with free open source software projects and non-profit organizations. I have listed a few of those below.

Arch Linux

Site: https://archlinux.org

Arch Linux is a lightweight Linux distribution with a fantastic community and great documentation. I try to help out where I can by adding to documentation, walking new users through installations, creating packages and building up the community.

Arch Linux Women

Site: http://archwomen.org

I founded Arch Linux Women in Febuary of 2012 to network with other female Arch users and encourage more women to use and contribute to Arch Linux. The experiance so far has been fantastic and I've gotten to know some amazing people through this project.

Wikimedia Foundation

Site: Wikimedia Foundation

I contribute to many of Wikimedia's projects, including wikidictionary, wikipedia, mediawiki and wikibooks. I edit articles, upload images, vote in referendums and contribute to their yearly fund raising efforts.
